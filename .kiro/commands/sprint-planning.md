---
description: "Action: Facilitate a sprint planning session to define the upcoming sprint's goals and backlog grooming to maintain a healthy product backlog."
---
Scope
• Reviewing and prioritizing items in the product backlog.
• Selecting user stories and tasks for the upcoming sprint.
• Estimating effort and defining a sprint goal.
• Assigning roles and responsibilities for the sprint.
• Reviewing team capacity and past performance.
• Identifying and mitigating potential risks or blockers.

Objective
To create a realistic and achievable plan for the upcoming sprint by selecting the most valuable backlog items, defining a clear sprint goal, and ensuring the team has a shared understanding of the work to be done. The process must:

• **Review Backlog**: Ensure backlog items are clear, prioritized, and ready for development.
• **Define Sprint Goal**: Establish a common objective for the sprint.
• **Select Backlog Items**: Choose items that align with the sprint goal and team capacity.
• **Estimate Effort**: Use a consistent method (e.g., story points, ideal time) to estimate the work.
• **Plan Capacity**: Consider team members' availability and existing commitments.
• **Identify Risks**: Anticipate potential obstacles and plan for them.

Deliverables
1.  A prioritized and refined product backlog.
2.  A defined Sprint Goal for the upcoming sprint.
3.  A set of selected backlog items (user stories, tasks) for the sprint.
4.  An effort estimate for each selected item and a total sprint commitment.
5.  A task board or sprint plan with initial assignments.
6.  A list of identified risks and mitigation strategies.

Process
1.  **Pre-Planning Preparation (1-2 days before the meeting)**:
    *   **Product Owner**: Reviews the top of the backlog, refines user stories (adds acceptance criteria, clarifies details), and prepares a prioritized list.
    *   **Scrum Master**: Sends the agenda and pre-read materials (backlog items, previous sprint review notes) to the team.
    *   **Development Team**: Reviews the pre-read materials and comes prepared with questions.

2.  **Sprint Planning Meeting (Typically 2-4 hours)**:
    *   **Part 1: Why are we building this? (1 hour)**
        *   The Product Owner discusses the product vision and the most valuable items in the backlog.
        *   The team asks questions to understand the "what" and "why" behind the items.
        *   The Product Owner and the team collaborate to define a Sprint Goal that is ambitious but achievable.

    *   **Part 2: How will we build this? (1-2 hours)**
        *   The Product Owner presents the top-priority backlog items.
        *   The Development Team asks technical questions and breaks down items into smaller tasks if necessary.
        *   The team estimates the effort for each item (e.g., using Planning Poker).
        *   The team selects a set of items they believe they can complete within the sprint, based on their capacity and the Sprint Goal.

    *   **Part 3: Who does what? (30 minutes - 1 hour)**
        *   The team discusses initial task assignments and creates a task board.
        *   The Scrum Master helps the team identify and document any potential risks or blockers.

3.  **Post-Planning Activities**:
    *   **Scrum Master**: Updates the project management tool (e.g., Jira, Trello) with the sprint plan and backlog items.
    *   **Product Owner**: Ensures the remaining backlog is kept up-to-date and ready for the next grooming session.
    *   **Development Team**: Begins work on the first items in the sprint backlog.

4.  **Backlog Grooming/Refinement (Ongoing, but dedicated time is set aside, e.g., 1-2 hours per week)**:
    *   The team reviews a portion of the backlog to ensure it is ready for future sprints.
    *   User stories are clarified, acceptance criteria are defined, and estimates are updated.
    *   The Product Owner prioritizes items based on business value and feedback.
    *   The goal is to have a "refined" backlog that is well-understood and estimated, reducing surprises during sprint planning.

Roles & Responsibilities
*   **Product Owner**: Responsible for the product backlog, defining the sprint goal, and maximizing the value of the work done by the development team.
*   **Scrum Master**: Facilitates the sprint planning meeting, ensures the team follows the agile process, and removes impediments.
*   **Development Team**: Self-organizes to select the work, estimate effort, and commit to completing the sprint goal.

Timeline
*   **Sprint Planning**: Held at the beginning of each sprint (e.g., every 1-2 weeks).
*   **Meeting Duration**: Typically 2-4 hours for a 2-week sprint.
*   **Backlog Grooming**: Held regularly (e.g., weekly) to keep the backlog healthy.
*   **Sprint Duration**: Fixed length (e.g., 1 or 2 weeks) to create a predictable rhythm.
